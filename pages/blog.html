How Technology Transformed Our Daily Life and Business

Technology has revolutionized every aspect of our lives. As recently as the 1990s, televisions were the height of technology in many American homes. According to the Census Bureau, a mere 15% of residences had a personal computer in 1989. By 2011, that number had climbed to 75% (JFG Inc). What used to be a luxury in the past has become a necessity, and it might be time to consider it abnormal for a household not to have computers.

This transformation is not just about the rise of computers; it extends to the internet, smartphones, and other smart devices. The internet has connected us in ways we could never have imagined before. Unlimited information at our fingertips, instant communication with anyone, anywhere in the world, and online shopping are just the gist of it.

Impact On Daily Life

To start off, one of the areas where technology has made the biggest impact is in the realm of communication. Conversing with people outside of your immediate area was once a difficult and lengthy process. This is not the case anymore, thanks to technological advancements. Smartphones, instant messaging, and social media platforms have made it possible to stay connected with friends and family, no matter where they are in the world, almost instantaneously.

Similarly, the role of technology in the medical field cannot go unnoticed. According to Interplex's article "How Medical Technology Advances Extend Life Expectancy," medical science and technology have been advancing each year, resulting in healthier, longer lifespans, and better medical treatment options. The average life expectancy has increased from 45 years in 1850 to almost 80 years in recent years, as a result of medical progress. Beyond complex medical diagnoses and treatments, technology is integrated into our daily health routines through fitness trackers, telemedicine, and health apps, making it easier to maintain and monitor our health. Furthermore, technology plays a crucial role in our daily convenience and comfort. From ordering food online when you crave something specific, to simple tasks like playing music while you clean your room with a vacuum cleaner, technology is deeply embedded in everyday activities. Smart home devices, online shopping, and streaming services have all become integral parts of modern life, streamlining daily chores and providing entertainment and services at our fingertips.

Overall, technology's influence on daily life is extensive, enhancing how we communicate, stay healthy, and manage everyday tasks, ultimately improving our overall quality of life.

Impact on Business

In the realm of business, technology has initiated a new era of innovation and competition. When you think of technology used in businesses, perhaps complicated robots in car assembly plants come to mind. However, it doesn't have to be that complicated. From simple accounting systems to point-of-sale systems, and even a basic calculator, technology is what has made these advancements possible.

Technology streamlines operations, enhances efficiency, and reduces the likelihood of human error. What used to be a lengthy process of keeping customer and accounting records in a register book is now a simple entry into a database that instantly provides the information you need.

Moreover, when you think of the most successful businesses in the world, it's often due to how well they have integrated technology into their operations. Airbnb doesn't own a single hotel, yet it is the largest accommodation provider globally. Uber doesn't own a single car, yet it is the largest ride-sharing company. The highest-valued companies today are mostly tech giants, such as Apple, Amazon, Meta, and Alphabet. These companies have leveraged technology to create innovative business models, streamline their operations, and provide unparalleled services, setting new standards in their respective industries.

Additionally, remote work and collaboration tools, such as video conferencing software and project management applications, have enabled businesses to outsource work to countries where living expenses are lower, resulting in substantially reduced wage costs. This not only helps companies save money but also provides opportunities for competent individuals who may have difficulty finding local employment to contribute to the global economy. Through these technologies, businesses can access a diverse talent pool and operate more flexibly and efficiently.
